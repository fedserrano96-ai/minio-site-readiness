\n A.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n For purposes of this Chapter, the following definitions shall apply:\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 1.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Coastal Bluff shall mean a steep escarpment with a slope gradient equal to or greater than an average\n of one foot vertical to one foot horizontal and a vertical rise of 15 feet or more,\n and which is located in an area that is periodically subject to ocean wave action.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 2.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Coastal Bluff Top shall mean the top edge of a coastal bluff as delineated using the following criteria:\n

\n \t\t\n

\n a.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n In cases where the coastal bluff involves a series of stepped vertical and horizontal\n planes, the bluff top shall be considered to start at the seaward edge of the topmost\n riser of the vertical plane of the coastal bluff.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n b.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n In cases where the coastal bluff edge is composed of a continuous, rather than stepped,\n downward slope, the bluff top shall be considered to start at that point nearest the\n bluff, seaward of which the downward gradient of the land surface increases more or\n less continuously at an angle which is more vertical than horizontal until it reaches\n the general gradient of the bluff.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 3.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Top Edge of Coastal Bluff shall mean the same as \"Coastal Bluff Top.\"\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 4.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Principal Structure shall mean a building or structure in which the primary use of the lot on which the\n building is located is being conducted.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 5.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Accessory Structure shall mean structures accessory or incidental to the principal structures on lot\n including, but not limited to, pools, spas, storage sheds, gazebos, and above-grade\n decks or patios.\n

\n A.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n In addition to the other provisions of the Del Mar Municipal Code, projects located\n within the Coastal Bluff Overlay Zone shall be subject to the following provisions:\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 1.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n All projects involving grading shall be subject to the submittal of an erosion and\n sedimentation control plan. Said plan shall ensure that all drainage from the impervious\n surfaces of the site will be collected and appropriately discharged in a manner which\n will prevent drainage and/or erosion related damage to the coastal bluff or any other\n properties or improvements in the vicinity. In addition, the plan shall include the\n provision of drainage facilities to convey all drainage away from any coastal bluff\n face and, where available, into existing developed storm drain systems capable of\n handling all anticipated drainage flows associated with the proposed project. Where\n an existing storm drain system is not available, the plan shall provide that drainage\n will be conveyed to a clearly defined, legal natural drainage course which can be\n shown to have adequate capacity to handle all required drainage flows without adverse\n impact to coastal bluffs. All designs and plans for drainage improvements shall be\n prepared by a licensed civil engineer and shall be subject to the review and approval\n of the City Engineer.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 2.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n All projects requiring a Conditional Use Permit and a Coastal Development Permit pursuant\n to the provisions of this Chapter shall be subject to the submittal of a detailed\n landscape plan. Said plan shall ensure that native and other drought-tolerant plant\n species will be utilized in a manner that will minimize irrigation requirements and\n reduce the potential of slide hazards due to over watering of the bluffs. Said plan\n will ensure that no new irrigation systems will be installed within 40 feet of the\n edge of the coastal bluff top and that any existing irrigation systems located within\n said 40 foot bluff top setback will be removed as part of project implementation.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 3.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n All projects involving grading shall be subject to the submittal of an erosion and\n sedimentation control plan. Said plan shall ensure that the project will not result\n in an increase in peak runoff from the site over the greatest discharge expected during\n a ten-year, six-hour frequency storm. Runoff control shall be accomplished by a variety\n of measures including, but not limited to, temporary and/or permanent on-site catchment\n basins, detention basins, siltation traps, energy dissipaters and the installation\n of landscape material. The required erosion and sedimentation control plan and any\n proposals to increase flows shall be subject to review and approval of the City Engineer.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 4.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n All temporary erosion control measures proposed or required pursuant to the provisions\n of this Section including berms, interceptor ditches, sandbagging, hay bales, filtered\n inlets, debris basins, silt traps, or other similar measures shall be installed prior\n to the commencement of grading in the areas for which the erosion control measures\n are intended.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 5.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n For projects involving a total of more than 25 cubic yards of cut and/or fill grading,\n no grading shall occur during the rainy season, identified for purposes of this Chapter\n as the period from November 15th to March 31st.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 6.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n In addition to other erosion control measures required pursuant to this Chapter, all\n graded slopes shall be stabilized prior to the November 15th onset of the rainy season\n through the provision of vegetative erosion control. Vegetative erosion control may\n be achieved through measures such as: landscape planting, seeding, mulching, fertilization,\n and irrigation. The installation of vegetative erosion control shall occur with sufficient\n time to achieve landscape coverage prior to the November 15th start of the rainy season.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n 7.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n All permits shall be subject to the submittal of a polluted runoff control plan. The\n required plan shall incorporate the use of structural and non-structural Best Management\n Practices (BMPs), to the extent necessary, to minimize the discharge of pollutants\n carried by runoff from urban development into surface water drainage, and to maintain\n post-development peak runoff rate and average volume at levels similar to pre-development\n levels. The plan shall include, but not be limited to the following Best Management\n Practices (BMPs), as applicable: silt traps, catch basins, oil/grit separators, street\n sweeping and cleaning program, low-maintenance landscape and pesticide management\n plan, solid waste management and public education program. Post-construction structural\n BMPs (or suites of BMPs) should be designed to treat, infiltrate or filter storm water\n runoff from each storm, up to and including the 85\n \n th\n \n percentile, 24-hour storm event for volume-based BMPs, and/or the 85\n \n th\n \n percentile, one-hour storm event, with an appropriate safety factor, for flow-based\n BMPs. The plan shall include a monitoring component to ensure long-term maintenance\n of BMPs as relevant, and to allow for continued evaluation of the effectiveness of\n the polluted runoff control plan in meeting the goals of LUP regarding the protection\n and enhancement of sensitive resources.\n
