23.15.040 - Requirements for Installation of Manufactured Homes.
","Id":"TIT23BUCO_CH23.15MAHO_23.15.040REINMAHO","Title":"23.15.040 - Requirements for Installation of Manufactured Homes.","Content":"
\n

\n In lieu of a home installed by conventional construction, the placement of a manufactured\n home on a lot within a permitted zone identified in Section 23.15.030 of this Chapter shall be permitted if done in compliance with the following requirements:\n \n

\n A.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Required Certification. At the time of application for installation of a manufactured\n home, the applicant shall demonstrate that the manufactured home meets federal certification\n standards in accordance with the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety\n Standards Act of 1974.\n

\n \t\t\n \n

\n B.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Permanent Foundation. Pursuant to Section 18551 of the California Health and Safety\n Code, the manufactured home must be installed on a permanent foundation system.\n

\n \t\t\n \n

\n C.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Building Permit Required. Prior to installing a manufactured home on a permanent foundation\n system, the manufactured home owner or a licensed contractor shall obtain a building\n permit from the Planning & Community Development Department in compliance with the\n requirements set forth in Section 18551 of the California Health and Safety Code and\n the local process for review and issuance of a building permit as set forth in Section\n 23.05.030.\n

\n \t\t\n \n

\n D.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Age Limitation. No more than ten (10) years may have elapsed between the manufacture\n date of a manufactured home and the application date for a building permit to install\n the manufactured home on a permanent foundation.\n

\n \t\t\n \n

\n E.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Compliance with Applicable Ordinances. All ordinances applicable to conventional single\n dwelling units within the applicable zone in which a manufactured home is located\n shall also apply to manufactured homes, including, but not limited to, requirements\n for utility connections including water and sewer, height, lot coverage, setbacks,\n parking, and other residential development standards.\n

\n \t\t\n \n

\n F.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Hazard Control and Risk Reduction. The manufactured home placement shall be subject\n to the same hazard control and risk reduction standards as any conventional single\n dwelling unit that would be placed on the same lot.\n

\n \t\t\n \n

\n G.\n

\n \t\t\n

\n Architectural Review Requirements Specific to Manufactured Homes. Manufactured homes\n shall be subject to the same architectural standards as conventional single dwelling\n units and look substantially like a conventional single dwelling unit as to its roof\n overhang, roofing material, siding material, parking and zoning requirements. Proposed\n manufactured homes and conventional single dwelling units are subject to a discretionary\n design review permit process in accordance with Chapter 23.08, unless otherwise specified per applicable law that a new dwelling unit on the lot\n is subject only to ministerial processes.\n

\n \t\t\n \n

\n (Ord. No. 1000, § 2, 9-18-2023)\n
